###  AI
**Definition**:

1. branch of computer science that develops **machines and software with intelligence**.

2. study of how to make computers do things which, at the moment, people do better.

   

**Intelligent agent**:

A system that **perceives its environment** and takes actions that **maximize its chances of success**.

- perceives its environment:

  Observation and understanding of the environment

  - Observations = > Facts (machine learning, pattern recognition)
  - Facts = > knowledge (data mining, knowledge representation, searching)

**Categories**:

- think - think humanly
-  act - act humanly



**Requirements**:

- *Self-awareness*: 

  being conscious of one’s own existence

- *Intentionality*: 

  having the intention of doing something, to achieve some goal

### The Turing Test

**target**: designed to provide a satisfactory operational definition of **intelligence**.

**demand**: 2 people and a machine

- The interrogator’s task: to find out which candidate is the machine or human, only by **asking them** **questions**. 
- The aim of the machine is to **fool** the interrogator into thinking that it is a person.
- If the machine can fool the interrogator **30% of the** **time**, the machine is considered **intelligent**.
